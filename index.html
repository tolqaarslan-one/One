Your Guide

Ankara is the capital city of Turkey and is located in the central part of the country. It has a population of over 5 million people, making it the second-largest city in Turkey after Istanbul. The city has a rich history dating back to ancient times and has been a cultural and political hub for centuries. It was first established by the Galatians in the 3rd century BC and was later ruled by the Romans, Byzantines, Seljuks, and Ottomans. One of the most famous landmarks in Ankara is the Anitkabir, which is the mausoleum of the founder of the Turkish Republic, Mustafa Kemal Ataturk. It is located on a hill overlooking the city and is a popular tourist attraction. Another important landmark is the Kocatepe Mosque, which is one of the largest mosques in Turkey and can accommodate over 20,000 worshippers at a time. The mosque has a unique modern design and is a popular spot for visitors. Ankara is also home to several museums, including the Museum of Anatolian Civilizations, which houses artifacts from various civilizations that once inhabited the region. The city has a vibrant cultural scene and hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, including the Ankara International Film Festival, Ankara Music Festival, and Ankara International Jazz Festival. Overall, Ankara is a fascinating city with a rich history, cultural heritage, and modern amenities that make it a great destination for tourists and visitors.
